How to Do This Correctly (Don't Screw It Up)

Balance transfer cards are powerful tools, but they have sharp edges. If you misuse them, you'll end up in deeper debt. Here is the playbook.

The 3 Golden Rules

  • 1. Stop using the card: Do not use a balance transfer card for new purchases. New purchases often accrue interest immediately, which ruins the math. Put the card in a drawer.
  • 2. Set Autopay: If you miss a single payment, the bank can revoke your 0% promo APR and hike it to 29.99% instantly. Set up autopay for at least the minimum, but ideally more.
  • 3. Beat the Clock: Divide your debt by the number of months (e.g., $5,000 / 21 months = $238/mo). Treat that number as your strict monthly bill. If you still have a balance when the 21 months are up, you will get hit with interest.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

Can I transfer debt from the same bank?

No. For example, you cannot transfer a balance from a Chase card to another Chase card. You must move debt between different banks (e.g., Chase -> Citi).

Does applying hurt my credit score?

Temporarily, yes. You will see a small dip (5-10 points) due to the hard inquiry. However, your score will likely shoot up later because your "Credit Utilization" will drop once you pay down the balance.
