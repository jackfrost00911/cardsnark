OUR RATING (Best All-Rounder)

Note: This card used to be called the "Citi Premier." Citi rebranded it to "Strata Premier" recently. It's the same card, just with a cooler name (allegedly).

Most travel cards have a blind spot. The Chase Sapphire Preferred is great for dining but terrible for groceries. The Amex Gold is great for groceries but terrible for gas. The Citi Strata Premier has no blind spots. It earns 3x points on Dining, Supermarkets, Gas Stations, Air Travel, and Hotels. It covers the entire daily life of a normal human being for a reasonable $95 fee.

The "Gas Station" Advantage

This is the killer feature.

The Chase Sapphire Preferred earns 2x on travel but 0x on gas. The Amex Gold earns 4x on food but 0x on gas.

The Citi Strata Premier earns 3x on Gas Stations. If you commute, drive a truck, or just enjoy road trips, this card fits your wallet better than the competition. It also includes EV Charging Stations in that 3x category.

The "Citi Trifecta" (Power User Move)

  • Strata Premier: Use it for Gas, Grocery, Dining, Travel (3x).
  • Double Cash: Use it for literally everything else (2x).
  • The Result: You never earn less than 2x points on any purchase, ever. And you pool all the points together to transfer to airlines. It is the simplest, most powerful two-card setup in the game.

Who Should Get This Card?

Get it if:

  • You spend money on Gas AND Groceries AND Dining (The Suburban Trifecta).
  • You want one card that does it all for a low fee.
  • You know how to transfer points to partners like Virgin Atlantic or Avianca.

Skip it if:

  • You want travel insurance (Trip delay, lost bags). Citi doesn't offer it on this card anymore. If you travel frequently, get the Chase Sapphire Preferred for the protections alone.
  • You only want cash back (Get the Blue Cash Preferred for 6% on groceries instead).

Bottom Line

The Citi Strata Premier is the most underrated card on the market. It doesn't get as much hype as Chase or Amex because it's not "sexy." But look at the math: 3x on Gas, Grocery, Dining, and Travel covers 80% of most people's spending.

For $95, it is an absolute workhorse.

Our verdict: 4.7/5. The best earning rates for the price.
