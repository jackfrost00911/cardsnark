OUR RATING (Best Luxury Business Card)

The Amex Business Platinum is less of a credit card and more of a coupon book made of heavy metal. It costs an eye-watering $695 a year. For that price, you get access to the best airport lounges in the world, elite status at hotels, and enough statement credits to make your accountant's head spin. If you travel for business, it's a tax-deductible necessity. If you don't travel, it's a very expensive paperweight.

The 35% Rebate (The Secret Weapon)

This is the feature that separates the Business Platinum from the Personal Platinum.

If you use Membership Rewards points to book a flight on your selected airline (or ANY business/first class flight) through Amex Travel, you get 35% of those points back.

‚ö†Ô∏è Why this matters

Normally, 100,000 points gets you $1,000 of travel. With this rebate, 100,000 points gets you roughly $1,540 of travel. It effectively makes your points worth 1.54 cents each, turning this into a powerful travel currency without dealing with transfer partners.

Lounge Access: Is it worth it?

If you fly more than 5 times a year, yes.

Airport food is expensive. A burger and a beer cost $40. The Centurion Lounge gives you a chef-curated buffet, craft cocktails, espresso, and fast Wi-Fi for free.

You also get a Priority Pass Select membership (1,200+ lounges worldwide) and access to Delta SkyClubs (when flying Delta). You will never sit at a gate again.

Business Platinum vs. Personal Platinum

They look the same, but the benefits are very different.

  • Business Plat: $400 Dell Credit, $120 Wireless Credit, $150 Adobe Credit, 35% Points Rebate. (Better for work expenses).
  • Personal Plat: $200 Uber Cash, $240 Streaming Credit, $200 Hotel Credit, $100 Saks Credit. (Better for lifestyle).
  • The Verdict: Get the Business version if you want the 35% rebate and Dell credits. Get the Personal version if you want Uber credits.

Who Should Get This Card?

Get it if:

  • You travel for business at least 4-5 times a year.
  • You buy computers/electronics from Dell (or can resell them).
  • You want elite status at Hilton and Marriott without staying 50 nights.
  • You have a huge tax bill (You can earn 1.5x points on purchases over $5,000).

Skip it if:

  • You rarely fly.
  • You find "coupon clipping" (tracking credits) annoying.
  • You want a card for everyday spending (The 1x rate is terrible).
