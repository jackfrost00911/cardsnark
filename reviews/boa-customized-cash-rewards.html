The "Online Shopping" Cheat Code

Most cards give you points for travel or dining. Very few cards give you a bonus for buying random junk on the internet.

If you set your 3% category to "Online Shopping," almost anything you buy through a web browser counts.

  • Ordering online for in-store pickup at Target? 3%.
  • Buying plane tickets on Expedia? 3%.
  • Buying clothes on Zara/H&M apps? 3%.
  • Food delivery apps (sometimes count as online shopping)? 3%.

The "Preferred Rewards" Multiplier

This is where Bank of America loyalists win big. If you keep money in BofA checking/savings accounts or Merrill investment accounts, your rewards get boosted.

  • Platinum Honors ($100k balance): 75% bonus (Your 3% becomes 5.25%).

Translation: If you have $100k in a Merrill IRA, this is the best cash back card in existence, period.

Who Should Get This Card?

Get it if:

  • You do a lot of online shopping (Amazon, apps, pickup orders).
  • You have variable spending (buying furniture one month, traveling the next).
  • You have substantial savings/investments with Bank of America or Merrill.

Skip it if:

  • You spend more than $2,500 per quarter (about $833/month) in your chosen category (You'll hit the cap).
  • You hate logging in to banking apps to manage settings.
  • You want premium travel perks like lounges.

Bottom Line

The Bank of America Customized Cash Rewards card is the ultimate "Gap Filler." Use your Amex Gold for food. Use your Venture X for travel. Use this card for "Online Shopping" or "Gas" or whatever category your other cards miss.

It fits into any wallet strategy perfectly because it can change to be whatever you need it to be.
