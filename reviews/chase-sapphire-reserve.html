How You Earn Points

The 3x Categories (The Daily Driver)

  • Travel: This is Chase's superpower. "Travel" is defined loosely. Flights, hotels, Airbnbs, campgrounds, cruise lines, trains, buses, taxis, Ubers, toll bridges, parking garages. If it moves you, it probably counts.
  • Dining: Restaurants, bars, breweries, cafes, delivery.

The 10x Portal Categories

Hotels & Car Rentals: Earn 10x points if you book through the Chase Ultimate Rewards portal. Flight bookings earn 5x in the portal.

Earning 10x sounds amazing, but be careful booking hotels in the portal. You usually won't earn Marriott/Hilton points or get status benefits if you book through Chase. For boutique hotels? Go for it.

The Secret Weapon: The 50% Boost

This is why people keep this card. Chase Ultimate Rewards points are generally worth 1 cent each.

With the Sapphire Reserve: Your points are worth 1.5 cents each when you use them to book travel.

Sapphire Reserve vs. Sapphire Preferred

The eternal debate. The Preferred is $95. The Reserve is $550. Here is the breakdown:

  • Get the Reserve ($550) if: You spend at least $300/year on travel AND you want lounge access. The "effective" fee becomes $250, and the extra points + lounge make it worth it.
  • Get the Preferred ($95) if: You want the sign-up bonus but don't care about lounges, or you want to keep your annual fees low.

Bottom Line: Is the CSR Worth It?

If you travel at least twice a year, yes. The $300 credit is so easy to use it's basically cash. That drops the fee to $250. For $250, getting Priority Pass lounges, 3x on all travel/dining, and the 50% point boost is a steal.

Get it if: You travel internationally, use Lyft/Uber, and hate sitting at airport gates.

Skip it if: You are strictly a budget traveler who never checks bags and eats packed sandwiches at the gate (respect, but this card isn't for you).
