The "Business" Loophole

We say this on every business card review, but it bears repeating: You do not need to be a Fortune 500 CEO to get this card.

Do you sell things on Etsy? Do you freelance? Do you tutor? Apply as a Sole Proprietor using your SSN. Your "Business Name" is just your own name. Chase loves sole proprietors.

Why 1.5% is Better Than You Think

"But CardSnark," you say, "My Citi Double Cash gets 2%. Why would I take 1.5%?"

Two reasons:

  1. The Bonus: This card gives you $750+ just for signing up. It would take you spending $150,000 on a 2% card to beat that head start.
  2. The "Trifecta" Play: If you also have a Chase Sapphire Preferred or Ink Preferred, you can move the cash back from this card to that card. Suddenly, your 1.5% cash back becomes 1.5x Ultimate Rewards points, which are worth way more when redeemed for Hyatt or United flights.

0% APR: The Inventory Hack

The Ink Unlimited comes with 0% Intro APR for 12 months on purchases.

If you need to buy $5,000 of inventory, a new laptop, or a camera for your business, you can put it on this card and pay it off slowly over a year without paying a dime in interest. Just make sure you pay it off before the year ends, or the interest will eat you alive.

Ink Unlimited vs. Ink Cash

Chase has two no-fee business cards. Which one is for you?

  • Get the Ink Unlimited if: Your spending is all over the place (Contractors, Inventory, Rent, Taxes). You just want one card for everything.
  • Get the Ink Cash if: You spend a lot on Office Supply Stores (5x) or Internet/Phone bills (5x).
  • Pro Move: Get both. They both have bonuses. They both have $0 fees. Why choose?
