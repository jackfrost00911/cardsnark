Active Cash vs. Citi Double Cash

This is the heavyweight title fight of the boring credit card world.

  • The Similarities: Both earn 2% on everything. Both have $0 annual fees.
  • Where Wells Fargo Wins: The Active Cash usually has a $200 Sign-Up Bonus. The Citi Double Cash rarely offers a bonus. That puts Wells Fargo $200 ahead on day one.
  • Where Wells Fargo Wins Again: Cell Phone Protection. Citi stripped almost all insurance benefits from their cards. Wells Fargo covers your phone.
  • Where Citi Wins: You can transfer Double Cash points to airlines (if you have a Premier/Strata card). Wells Fargo rewards are pretty much just cash.

The Cell Phone Protection (Hidden Gem)

This is rare for a no-fee card. If you pay your monthly cell phone bill with the Active Cash, you get up to $600 of protection against damage or theft.

There is a $25 deductible, which is peanuts compared to the cost of a new iPhone screen. This benefit alone can save you $15/month on AppleCare or carrier insurance.

Who Should Get This Card?

Get it if:

  • You want one card for everything and hate tracking categories.
  • You want a $200 bonus for very little spending ($500).
  • You pay for cell phone insurance and want to stop paying for it.
  • You need to make a large purchase (furniture/appliances) and want 15 months of 0% interest.

Skip it if:

  • You travel internationally often (The 3% foreign fee kills the rewards).
  • You are a "maximizer" who wants 3x or 4x points on dining and travel.
  • You hate having to wait until you earn $25 to redeem your cash.
