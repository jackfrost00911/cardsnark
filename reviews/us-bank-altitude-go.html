The U.S. Bank Altitude Go is the best-kept secret in credit cards. The 4x Dining "Glitch"

Usually, to get 4x or 4% back on dining, you have to pay an annual fee (like the Amex Gold). The Altitude Go gives it to you for free.

What counts? Everything you put in your mouth that you didn't cook yourself.

  • Sit-down restaurants
  • Fast food drive-thrus
  • Bars and Breweries
  • Uber Eats / DoorDash / Grubhub

The $15 Streaming Credit

This is a quirky benefit, but it's free money.

If you put a monthly streaming subscription on this card (Netflix, Hulu, Spotify, Apple Music, Disney+, etc.) for 11 consecutive months, U.S. Bank gives you a $15 statement credit.

Basically, they buy you one month of Netflix every year just for using their card.

Altitude Go vs. SavorOne

This is the main showdown. Both have $0 annual fees.

  • Altitude Go: Wins on Dining (4x vs 3x).
  • SavorOne: Wins on Groceries & Entertainment (3x vs 2x).
  • The Verdict: If you cook at home a lot, get the SavorOne. If you eat out constantly (or order delivery), get the Altitude Go.

Who Should Get This Card?

Get it if:

  • You spend a lot on food/drinks but hate annual fees.
  • You travel internationally (No foreign transaction fees makes this a great backup card).
  • You want a metal card to impress your friends (it is weirdly heavy for a free card).

Skip it if:

  • You want airline miles (Chase/Amex are better for that).
  • You spend way more on groceries than restaurants (This card is only 2x on groceries).
  • You have opened a lot of new credit cards recently (U.S. Bank hates "churners").

Bottom Line

The U.S. Bank Altitude Go is a specialist. It does one thing perfectly: It rewards you for eating. With 4x points and no annual fee, it is arguably the most valuable dining card in existence for budget-conscious foodies.

Our verdict: 4.8/5. The best dining card you've never heard of.
