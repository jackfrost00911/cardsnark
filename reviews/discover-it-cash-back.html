How You Earn Cash Back (With Actual Examples)

Rotating 5% Categories

Every quarter, Discover announces two 5% categories. You must activate them (takes 30 seconds in the app) to earn the bonus. Forget to activate? You're earning 1% like a chump.

Typical Annual Category Calendar:

  • Q1 (Jan-Mar): Grocery stores, drugstores, Walgreens, CVS
  • Q2 (Apr-Jun): Gas stations, wholesale clubs, Costco, Sam's Club
  • Q3 (Jul-Sep): Restaurants, PayPal
  • Q4 (Oct-Dec): Amazon, Target, Walmart

*Note: Categories rotate annually and Discover announces them ahead of time.*

The 5% Spending Cap

You earn 5% on the first $1,500 spent per quarter in bonus categories. After that, it drops to 1%. That's a maximum of $75 cash back per quarter in bonus categories, or $300/year.

First Year Math (With Cashback Match)

Let's say you spend conservatively:

  • $1,500/quarter in 5% categories = $300 cash back
  • $500/month on everything else = $60 cash back
  • Year one total: $360 earned
  • Cashback Match doubles it: +$360 bonus

Grand total year one: $720

That's $720 for free. No annual fee, no strings. Just for using this card instead of a debit card.

How to Activate Quarterly Categories (Don't Forget This!)

Here's how:

  1. Log into the Discover app or website
  2. Look for the "Activate 5% Cash Back" banner

Who Should Get This Card

Perfect for:

  • First-time credit card users (easy approval + doubled rewards = perfect start)
  • Students building credit (no annual fee, forgiving approval)
  • People who can remember to activate quarterly (set a reminder!)
  • Anyone who shops at Amazon, Target, or gas stations regularly
  • Category optimization nerds who want to pair this with a flat-rate card

Skip it if:

  • You shop at Costco frequently (Discover isn't accepted there)
  • You travel internationally often (acceptance is spotty)
  • You'll forget to activate categories every quarter (you'll earn 1% instead of 5%)
  • You want a single card for everything (pair with a 2% flat-rate card instead)
  • You already have 5+ credit cards (you might not get approved)

How to Maximize This Card (The Pro Strategy)

Year One: Go All-In

Since Discover doubles everything, use this card for EVERYTHING in year one (as long as it's accepted). You're effectively earning 2% on all purchases and 10% on bonus categories. No other card comes close.

Year Two: Pair with a Flat-Rate Card

After year one, the Cashback Match ends. Now use Discover only for 5% categories and switch to a 2% flat-rate card (Citi Double Cash, Wells Fargo Active Cash) for everything else.

Optimal Card Combo:

  • Discover it Cash Back for 5% rotating categories ($75/quarter max)
  • Citi Double Cash for 2% on everything else
  • Total setup cost: $0 annual fees
  • Total effort: Activate Discover quarterly, use Citi for default

This combination covers 90% of spending at 2-5% back with zero annual fees. Simple, effective, free.

Bottom Line: Is Discover it Cash Back Worth It?

For first-year cardholders? Absolutely yes. The Cashback Match makes this the single best card for anyone new to credit cards or anyone willing to put all their spending on it for 12 months.

After year one? Still good, but not as dominant. It becomes a solid 5% rotating category card that you pair with a flat-rate card for maximum earnings.

Get it if: You're building credit, you want maximum first-year rewards, and you can remember to activate categories.

Skip it if: You shop at Costco, travel internationally often, or will forget to activate.
