The "5% Fee" Debate

This is the most important part of the review. Most balance transfer cards charge a 3% fee to move your debt. The Wells Fargo Reflect often charges a 5% fee (check the current terms, sometimes there is an intro offer for 3%, but 5% is common).

Reflect vs. Simplicity

These are the two titans of the 21-month offer.

  • Citi Simplicity: 21 months 0% APR. No Late Fees. 3% transfer fee (usually).
  • Wells Fargo Reflect: 21 months 0% APR (if on time). Cell Phone Protection. 5% transfer fee (usually).
  • The Winner: Citi Simplicity usually wins on math (lower fee). Wells Fargo wins on perks (phone insurance).

Who Should Get This Card?

Get it if:

  • You need the absolute maximum time possible (21 months) to pay off debt.
  • You want cell phone insurance but don't want to pay an annual fee.
  • You already bank with Wells Fargo (easier approval/management).

Skip it if:

Bottom Line

The Wells Fargo Reflect is a specialized tool. It buys you time. A LOT of time. If you are staring at a mountain of debt and just need the interest to stop so you can catch your breath, this card pauses the clock for nearly two years. That peace of mind is often worth the transfer fee.
