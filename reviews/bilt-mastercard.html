How the "Rent" Thing Actually Works

Usually, if you pay rent with a credit card, you get hit with a 3% "convenience fee." That wipes out any rewards you earn.

Bilt is smarter. They give you a unique Routing Number and Account Number (like a bank account). You plug these numbers into your landlord's payment portal. To your landlord, it looks like an ACH bank transfer. To you, it's a credit card charge earning points.

Does your landlord only take checks? Bilt will literally print a paper check and mail it to your landlord via USPS for free. You still get the points.

The "5 Times" Rule (Don't Screw This Up)

This is the only catch, and it's a big one. To earn your rent points, you must make 5 transactions on the card every statement period.

The Strategy: Your rent payment counts as 1. You need 4 more. Buy a coffee, pay your Netflix bill, buy a pack of gum. Just make sure you hit 5, or your 2,000 rent points vanish into the ether.

"Rent Day" (The First of the Month)

On the 1st of every month (Rent Day), Bilt doubles the rewards on non-rent spend.

  • Dining: 6x points
  • Travel: 4x points
  • Everything Else: 2x points

Pro Tip: Don't pay for dinner on the 30th. Wait until the 1st. Book your flights on the 1st. It turns this no-fee card into a points-earning monster for 24 hours a month.
