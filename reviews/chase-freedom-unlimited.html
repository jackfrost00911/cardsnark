The Deep Dive

The flat-rate card that plays well with others. If you pair it with a Chase Sapphire Preferred or Reserve, your "1.5%" suddenly becomes worth 1.87-3+ cents per dollar spent.

The Chase Trifecta Strategy

This is why credit card nerds won't shut up about Chase. You combine three cards to maximize every dollar:

  • Chase Freedom Unlimited: 1.5% on everything (The Catch-All).
  • Chase Freedom Flex: 5% rotating categories (The Specialist).
  • Chase Sapphire Preferred: 25% redemption bonus & transfer partners (The Hub).

Earning Breakdown

  • 5% (5x points): Travel purchased through Chase Ultimate Rewards portal.
  • 3% (3x points): Dining and drugstores.
  • 1.5% (1.5x points): Everything else.

Why 1.5% Beats 2% (Sometimes)

Citi Double Cash gives you 2% flat cash back. Freedom Unlimited gives 1.5% in Chase Ultimate Rewards points. If you have a Sapphire Preferred, you can transfer those points to airline partners where they can be worth 3+ cents per dollar. That's why optimizers choose Freedom Unlimited over higher flat-rate cards.

Additional Perks

  • Cell Phone Protection: Pay your bill with this card, get up to $600 coverage ($50 deductible).
  • Purchase Protection: Covers new purchases against damage/theft for 120 days.
  • No Foreign Transaction Fees: The card charges $0 on foreign transactions.

The 5/24 Rule (Important!)

Chase has an unwritten rule: if you've opened 5+ credit cards (from ANY issuer) in the past 24 months, you're auto-denied. If you want Chase cards, get them early.
