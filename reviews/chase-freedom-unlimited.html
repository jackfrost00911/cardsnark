Chase Freedom Unlimited Review 2025

The flat-rate card that plays well with others

4.6/5

OUR RATING

The Chase Freedom Unlimited is the credit card equivalent of a reliable Honda Accord: not sexy, not flashy, but damn good at its job. It earns 1.5% on everything with zero annual fee, which makes it a solid standalone card. But pair it with a Chase Sapphire Preferred or Reserve, and suddenly you're earning transferable points that can be worth 2-3x more. That's the secret sauce: this isn't just a 1.5% cash back card‚Äîit's a Chase Ultimate Rewards points factory that feeds your travel card. Chase knows exactly what they're doing here, and if you're smart, you'll play along.

How You Earn Points (The Details)

Earning Breakdown

  • 5% (5x points): Travel purchased through Chase Ultimate Rewards portal
  • 3% (3x points): Dining (restaurants, delivery, takeout) + drugstores (CVS, Walgreens, etc.)
  • 1.5% (1.5x points): Everything else (groceries, gas, Amazon, Target, utilities, literally everything)

Real Earnings Example

Let's say you spend $2,000/month:

  • $500/month dining: 18,000 points/year (3x)

How to Redeem Points

Option 1: Cash Back (1 cent per point)

Redeem for statement credit, direct deposit, or check at 1 cent per point. Simple, but you're leaving value on the table.

Example: 66,800 points = $668 cash back

Option 2: Chase Travel Portal (1-1.25 cents per point)

  • Freedom card only: 1 cent/point
  • Sapphire Preferred: 1.25 cents/point
  • Sapphire Reserve: 1.5 cents/point

Example with Sapphire Preferred: 66,800 points = $835 in travel

Option 3: Transfer to Partners (1-3+ cents per point)

Transfer to airlines like United, Southwest, British Airways, or hotels like Hyatt. Requires a Sapphire or Ink card. Best value if you know what you're doing.

Bottom line: This card is fine alone, elite paired with Sapphire.

The 5/24 Rule (Important!)

Chase has an unwritten rule: if you've opened 5+ credit cards (from ANY issuer) in the past 24 months, you're auto-denied.

Why this matters: If you want Chase cards, get them early. Once you hit 5/24, you're locked out until cards fall off.

Strategy tip: Get Freedom Unlimited first, then Sapphire Preferred 3-6 months later. Both before you hit 5/24.

Who Should Get This Card

Perfect for:

  • Anyone already in the Chase ecosystem (or planning to be)
  • People who want a simple flat-rate card with no annual fee
  • Sapphire cardholders who want to maximize everyday spending
  • Beginners building credit (easy approval, no fee)
  • People who dine out regularly (3% is solid)

Skip it if:

  • You want 2% flat cash back (get Citi Double Cash instead)
  • You're over 5/24 (Chase will deny you)
  • You'll never get a Sapphire card (value is capped at 1.5%)
  • You want rotating 5% categories (get Freedom Flex instead)
