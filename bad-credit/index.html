How Secured Cards Actually Work

Think of a secured card like credit card training wheels. You give the bank a deposit (say, $200). They hold onto that money in case you disappear. In exchange, they give you a credit card with a $200 limit.

You use it for small purchases (Netflix, gum, gas) and pay it off every month. The bank tells the credit bureaus, "Hey, this person is responsible!" After 6-12 months, they usually upgrade you to a regular card and send your deposit back.
