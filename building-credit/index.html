How Secured Cards Actually Work

Think of a secured card like a security deposit on an apartment. You give the bank $200. They hold it in a locked safe. In exchange, they give you a credit card with a $200 limit.

The Graduation Process

  1. Open the card: Pay your deposit.
  2. Use it lightly: Buy one tank of gas per month.
  3. Pay in full: Pay the bill from your checking account.
  4. Get upgraded: After 6-12 months, the bank reviews your account. If you paid on time, they send your $200 back and switch you to a regular card.

FAQ: Frequently Asked Questions

Can I just use a debit card?

No. Debit cards do not report to credit bureaus. You could use a debit card for 50 years and still have a credit score of zero.

How long does it take?

If you start from scratch today, you will have a FICO score in about 6 months. By month 12, if you pay on time, your score could be over 700.
