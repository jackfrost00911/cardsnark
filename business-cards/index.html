Do You Actually Need a Business Card?

There is a myth that you need an LLC, a storefront, and 5 employees to get a business credit card. That is false.

Who Qualifies?

  • Freelancers: If you sell services (writing, design, consulting).
  • Gig Workers: Uber, DoorDash, TaskRabbit.
  • Online Sellers: eBay, Etsy, Poshmark.
  • Sole Proprietors: Anyone who makes money on the side.

üí° The Application Hack

When the application asks for "Legal Business Name," just use your own name.
When it asks for "Business Structure," choose Sole Proprietorship.
You use your Social Security Number (SSN) instead of an EIN. This is 100% legal and standard.

Business vs. Personal Cards

Why bother getting a separate card? Three reasons:

  1. Tax Season Sanity: Mixing personal and business expenses is a nightmare during tax season. Keep them separate and your accountant will love you.
  2. Credit Reporting: Most business cards (Amex, Chase, Citi) do not report to your personal credit report. This means you can run up a high balance for inventory without tanking your personal credit score.
  3. Higher Limits: Business cards often have limits of $20k, $50k, or more because banks know businesses have higher expenses.
