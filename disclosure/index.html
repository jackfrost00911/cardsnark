Affiliate Disclosure

How we keep the lights on without selling our souls.

Last Updated: October 4, 2025

How We Make Money

CardSnark is an independent credit card comparison and review website. We earn revenue through affiliate partnerships with credit card issuers and financial institutions.

Basically: When you click a link on our site and get approved for a card, the bank pays us a commission. It costs you nothing extra.

Affiliate Relationships

We maintain affiliate relationships with various providers, including:

  • Major banks (Chase, American Express, Citi, Discover, Capital One, etc.)
  • Regional banks and credit unions
  • Credit monitoring services
  • Financial comparison platforms

Editorial Independence

This is the important part: Our reviews are based on objective analysis. We evaluate cards based on rewards, fees, bonuses, and perks.

What We Do

We recommend cards that actually provide value. If a card sucks, we say it sucks, even if they would pay us to say otherwise.

What We Don't Do

We do not let banks review, approve, or edit our content before it is published. Our snark is 100% our own.

Not All Links Pay Us

Sometimes the best card on the market doesn't have an affiliate program. We recommend it anyway. Our goal is to be the most trustworthy resource on the internet, not the most profitable.

FTC Compliance

This disclosure complies with the Federal Trade Commission's requirements for affiliate relationships and endorsements (16 CFR Part 255). We believe in transparency because we respect your intelligence.
