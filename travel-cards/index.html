How to Choose a Travel Card (Without a Spreadsheet)

Travel credit cards are complicated on purpose. They want you to get distracted by "3X Points" and forget about the $500 annual fee. Here is the simple math to decide which tier you belong in.

Tier 1: The "Once a Year" Traveler ($0 - $95 Fee)

If you only fly home for Christmas and maybe take one vacation, do not get a luxury card. You won't use the perks.

  • Look for: Low annual fees and points that don't expire.
  • Avoid: Cards with "Statement Credits" that require you to buy things you don't need.
  • Best Pick: Chase Sapphire Preferred.

Tier 2: The "Digital Nomad" / Business Traveler ($250+ Fee)

If you are in an airport more than 5 times a year, lounge access becomes a mental health necessity, not a luxury.

  • Look for: Priority Pass Select (Lounge access) and TSA PreCheck credits.
  • Best Pick: Capital One Venture X (It effectively pays you to keep it).

The "Golden Rule" of Travel Points

Never redeem points for cash back on Amazon. That is how the banks win. 60,000 Chase points are worth $600 on Amazon, but they can be worth $1,200 if you transfer them to Hyatt or United Airlines.
