## The Deep Dive

This is the best value premium travel card on the market. Period. A $5 net annual fee for Priority Pass, $750 sign-up bonus, and 10x on hotels is borderline offensive to competitors charging $550-695.

### The Value Math (That Actually Makes Sense)
Capital One is basically paying YOU $5 to keep this card. Here is the breakdown:

* **Annual Fee:** $395
* **Annual Travel Credit:** -$300 (easy to use, any travel counts)
* **Anniversary Bonus:** -10,000 miles ($100 value)
* **Net Annual Cost:** <strong class="text-green-600">-$5</strong>

### How You Earn Miles
* **10x miles:** Hotels and rental cars booked through Capital One Travel.
* **5x miles:** Flights booked through Capital One Travel.
* **2x miles:** Everything else (groceries, gas, dining, Amazon, literally everything).

### The $300 Travel Credit
Unlike Amex's convoluted airline credit system, Capital One's is simple. The $300 credit applies to flights, hotels, rental cars, and more booked through the portal.

### Lounge Access (The Real Flex)
You get unlimited access to 1,300+ Priority Pass lounges worldwide and can bring two guests for free. Plus, you get access to Capital One's own luxury lounges (currently in Dallas, Denver, Washington DC), which feature premium food and drinks.

### Who Should Get This Card?
1.  Frequent travelers (4+ flights/year) who want lounge access.
2.  People who want premium perks without Amex Platinum pricing.
3.  Anyone who values simplicity (2x on everything, no categories).
