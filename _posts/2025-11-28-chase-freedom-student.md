    <h2>The Deep Dive</h2>
    <p>The Chase Freedom Student was once the "backdoor" entrance into the Chase ecosystem for students with thin credit files. While it offered a modest 1% cash back, its real value was building a history with Chase. However, Chase has officially retired this card for new applicants and replaced it with the superior <strong>Chase Freedom Rise</strong>.</p>

    <h3>For Existing Cardholders: Should You Keep It?</h3>
    <p><strong>Yes.</strong> There is no annual fee, so keeping it open helps your average age of credit. Plus, you get a <strong>$20 Good Standing Reward</strong> every year for your first 5 years. That's free money just for having the card.</p>

    <h3>The "Lyft" Benefit</h3>
    <p>Existing cardholders can still earn <strong>2% total cash back</strong> on Lyft rides through September 30, 2027. While not as high as the Sapphire Reserve's 10x, it's better than the standard 1%.</p>

    <h3>The Replacement: Chase Freedom Rise</h3>
    <p>If you are a student looking for a Chase card today, you want the <strong>Freedom Rise</strong>. Here is how they compare:</p>
    <ul>
        <li><strong>Freedom Student (Old):</strong> 1% Cash Back, $20 Annual Bonus.</li>
        <li><strong>Freedom Rise (New):</strong> 1.5% Cash Back, $25 Autopay Bonus.</li>
        <li><strong>The Winner:</strong> The Freedom Rise is mathematically better for spending.</li>
    </ul>
