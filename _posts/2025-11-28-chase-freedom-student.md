## The Deep Dive

The Chase Freedom Student was once the "backdoor" entrance into the Chase ecosystem for students with thin credit files. While it offered a modest 1% cash back, its real value was building a history with Chase. However, Chase has officially retired this card for new applicants and replaced it with the superior **Chase Freedom Rise**.

### For Existing Cardholders: Should You Keep It?
**Yes.** There is no annual fee, so keeping it open helps your average age of credit. Plus, you get a **$20 Good Standing Reward** every year for your first 5 years. That's free money just for having the card.

### The "Lyft" Benefit
Existing cardholders can still earn **2% total cash back** on Lyft rides through September 30, 2027. While not as high as the Sapphire Reserve's 10x, it's better than the standard 1%.

### The Replacement: Chase Freedom Rise
If you are a student looking for a Chase card today, you want the **Freedom Rise**. Here is how they compare:

* **Freedom Student (Old):** 1% Cash Back, $20 Annual Bonus.
* **Freedom Rise (New):** 1.5% Cash Back, $25 Autopay Bonus.
* **The Winner:** The Freedom Rise is mathematically better for spending.
