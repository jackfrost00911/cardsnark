    <h2>The Deep Dive</h2>
    <p>This is basically the "adult" Quicksilver card, but available to students with thin credit files. Getting 1.5% back on a student card with no annual fee is excellent.</p>

    <h3>The Offer (Simple Math)</h3>
    <ul>
        <li><strong>Annual Fee:</strong> $0.</li>
        <li><strong>Cash Back:</strong> 1.5% Unlimited.</li>
        <li><strong>Sign-up Bonus:</strong> $50 (after spending $100).</li>
        <li><strong>Uber One:</strong> 6 months free + 10% cash back on Uber/Uber Eats (thru Nov 2024).</li>
    </ul>

    <h3>The "Study Abroad" Champion</h3>
    <p>If you plan to study abroad in London, Barcelona, or Tokyo, this is the only card you should bring.</p>
    <ul>
        <li><strong>Chase Freedom Student:</strong> Charges 3% foreign fees.</li>
        <li><strong>Citi Rewards Student:</strong> Charges 3% foreign fees.</li>
        <li><strong>Discover Student:</strong> No fees, but Discover isn't accepted in many countries.</li>
        <li><strong>Quicksilver Student:</strong> No fees AND it's a Mastercard (accepted everywhere).</li>
    </ul>
    <p>Spending $5,000 during a semester abroad on a Chase card would cost you $150 in fees. On this card, it costs $0.</p>

    <h3>Quicksilver Student vs. SavorOne Student</h3>
    <p>Capital One actually has two student cards. The <strong>SavorOne Student</strong> earns 3% on Dining and Groceries, which is usually better for students who spend a lot on food. However, the <strong>Quicksilver Student</strong> earns 1.5% on <em>everything</em>, making it better if you want simplicity or spend money on random things like clothes and textbooks.</p>

    <h3>Who Should Get This Card?</h3>
    <ul>
        <li>You are planning to study or travel abroad.</li>
        <li>You want a simple card that earns more than 1%.</li>
        <li>You use Uber or Uber Eats frequently.</li>
    </ul>
