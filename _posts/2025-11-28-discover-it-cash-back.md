    <h2>The Deep Dive</h2>
    <p>The card that literally doubles your rewards for an entire year. Yes, you have to remember to activate quarterly 5% categories. But if you do, the math is unbeatable for a no-fee card.</p>

    <h3>Cashback Match: The Free Money Machine</h3>
    <p>Discover matches ALL the cash back you earn in your first year. Earned $300? Discover gives you another $300 at the end of year one. There is no cap.</p>
    <p><strong>Translation:</strong> Your 5% categories become 10%, your 1% spending becomes 2%, and you effectively double your rewards for doing absolutely nothing.</p>

    <h3>How You Earn Cash Back</h3>
    <p>Every quarter, Discover announces two 5% categories. You must activate them to earn the bonus. Typical categories include:</p>
    <ul>
        <li><strong>Q1 (Jan-Mar):</strong> Grocery stores, drugstores.</li>
        <li><strong>Q2 (Apr-Jun):</strong> Gas stations, wholesale clubs (but not Costco).</li>
        <li><strong>Q3 (Jul-Sep):</strong> Restaurants, PayPal.</li>
        <li><strong>Q4 (Oct-Dec):</strong> Amazon, Target, Walmart.</li>
    </ul>

    <h3>First Year Math (With Cashback Match)</h3>
    <p>Let's say you spend $1,500/quarter in 5% categories ($300 cash back) and $500/month on everything else ($60 cash back). That's $360 earned. Discover doubles it to <strong>$720 total</strong>. That's $720 for free just for using this card.</p>

    <h3>The Acceptance Problem</h3>
    <p>Discover is accepted at 99% of major US retailers, but there are gaps. It does <strong>not</strong> work at Costco. It works at most restaurants, gas stations, and online stores. Keep a Visa or Mastercard as a backup for international travel or Costco runs.</p>

    <h3>Who Should Get This Card?</h3>
    <ul>
        <li>First-time credit card users (easy approval + doubled rewards).</li>
        <li>Students building credit.</li>
        <li>Category optimization nerds who want to pair this with a flat-rate card.</li>
    </ul>
