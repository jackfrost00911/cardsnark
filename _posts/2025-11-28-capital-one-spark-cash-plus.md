<div class="text-container prose">
    <h2>The Deep Dive</h2>
    <p>The Spark Cash Plus is a power tool for big spenders. Unlimited 2% back on massive transaction volumes adds up incredibly fast. If your business has the cash flow to support it, this card is a profit machine.</p>

    <h3>The Math (Big Numbers Only)</h3>
    <p>If your business spends less than $150,000 a year, the $150 fee is annoying. If you spend MORE than $150k, this card is essentially free and earns uncapped 2% rewards.</p>
    <ul>
        <li><strong>Annual Fee:</strong> $150.</li>
        <li><strong>Sign-up Bonus:</strong> $1,200 (Requires $30k spend).</li>
        <li><strong>Fee Refund:</strong> Spend $150k/year, get the $150 fee back.</li>
    </ul>

    <h3>Charge Card vs. Credit Card</h3>
    <p>This is the most important distinction. A <strong>Credit Card</strong> has a limit and lets you pay minimums. A <strong>Charge Card</strong> (like this one) has no hard limit but requires full payment every month. If you run a company where you spend $50k+ a month and pay it off immediately, this card is a dream. If you are struggling with cash flow, this card is a nightmare.</p>

    <h3>The "Personal Credit" Problem</h3>
    <p>This is Capital One's biggest flaw for business owners. Unlike Chase or Amex, Capital One <strong>DOES</strong> report business activity to your personal credit report. If you run up a $40,000 balance for your business, your personal credit score might drop because it looks like YOU personally owe $40,000.</p>

    <h3>Who Should Get This Card?</h3>
    <ul>
        <li>Your business spends over $150,000 a year (Fee becomes $0).</li>
        <li>You need to make purchases larger than a typical credit limit allows.</li>
        <li>You want simple cash back instead of confusing travel points.</li>
    </ul>
