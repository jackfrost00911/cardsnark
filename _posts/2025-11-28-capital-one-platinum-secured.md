    <h2>The Deep Dive</h2>
    <p>The Capital One Platinum Secured isn't flashy. It doesn't offer rewards. But it is safe, reliable, and free to hold. It does exactly what it promises: it helps you build credit without ripping you off with hidden fees.</p>

    <h3>The Deposit (The Buy-In)</h3>
    <p>This is one of the only cards where your deposit might be <i>less</i> than your credit limit. If you qualify for the lowest tier, you can get a $200 credit line with just a $49 deposit.</p>
    <ul>
        <li><strong>Annual Fee:</strong> $0.</li>
        <li><strong>Minimum Deposit:</strong> $49, $99, or $200 (Depending on credit).</li>
        <li><strong>Credit Line:</strong> Usually starts at $200.</li>
    </ul>

    <h3>How "Secured" Cards Work</h3>
    <p>Think of a secured card like bowling with bumpers. You give the bank a deposit, which sits in a locked account. If you pay your bill, they report your good behavior to Experian, Equifax, and TransUnion. Once your score is good enough, you can upgrade to an unsecured card and get your deposit back.</p>

    <h3>The "Netflix Strategy" for Rebuilding</h3>
    <p>Getting a card with a $200 limit is annoying. So don't try to use it for everything.</p>
    <ol>
        <li>Put <strong>one</strong> small recurring bill on this card (like Netflix or Spotify).</li>
        <li>Set the card to <strong>Auto-Pay</strong> the full balance every month.</li>
        <li>Cut the card up or put it in a sock drawer.</li>
    </ol>
    <p><strong>The Result:</strong> Every month, the card reports "Paid on Time" and "Low Utilization" to the bureaus while you do literally nothing.</p>

    <h3>Who Should Get This Card?</h3>
    <ul>
        <li>You have "Bad" or "Poor" credit (Score below 600).</li>
        <li>You have no credit history at all (Student/Immigrant).</li>
        <li>You want a card with no annual fee to keep open long-term.</li>
    </ul>
