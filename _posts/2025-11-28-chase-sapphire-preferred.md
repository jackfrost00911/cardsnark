    <h2>The Deep Dive</h2>
    <p>If you are looking to get into the travel points game, this is where you start. The Chase Sapphire Preferred (CSP) is legendary for a reason: it's flexible.</p>

    <h3>How much are the points actually worth?</h3>
    <p>Chase advertises the bonus as $600 cash. <strong>Do not redeem them for cash.</strong> That is a rookie mistake.</p>
    <p>If you use the Chase Travel portal, they are worth <strong>$750</strong>. If you transfer them to Hyatt for a hotel stay, they could be worth <strong>$1,200+</strong>.</p>

    <h3>Who is this card for?</h3>
    <ul>
        <li>Travelers who want free flights but don't want a $500 annual fee card.</li>
        <li>People who spend a lot on dining (3x points).</li>
        <li>Anyone who rents cars (the primary insurance saves you ~$15/day).</li>
    </ul>

    <h3>Who should skip it?</h3>
    <p>If you never travel, get the <strong>Citi Double Cash</strong> instead. Paying $95 for travel points you won't use is just donating money to Chase.</p>
