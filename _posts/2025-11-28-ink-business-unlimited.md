    <h2>The Deep Dive</h2>
    <p>For business owners who are too busy to remember if "Office Supplies" earns 2x or 3x this month, the Ink Business Unlimited is the perfect solution.</p>

    <h3>The Barrier to Entry</h3>
    <p>The bonus is basically free money: Spend $6,000 on your business in 3 months, get $750 back. That is a 12.5% return on spend, which is insane for a no-fee card.</p>
    <ul>
        <li><strong>Annual Fee:</strong> $0.</li>
        <li><strong>Sign-up Bonus:</strong> $750 cash back (requires $6,000 spend).</li>
        <li><strong>Employee Cards:</strong> Free.</li>
    </ul>

    <h3>The "Business" Loophole</h3>
    <p>You do not need to be a Fortune 500 CEO to get this card. Do you sell things on Etsy? Do you freelance? Do you tutor? Apply as a <strong>Sole Proprietor</strong> using your Social Security Number. Your "Business Name" is just your own name. Chase loves sole proprietors.</p>

    <h3>Why 1.5% is Better Than You Think</h3>
    <p>Even though the Citi Double Cash gets 2%, the Ink Unlimited has two advantages:</p>
    <ol>
        <li><strong>The Bonus:</strong> This card gives you $750 just for signing up. It would take you spending $150,000 on a 2% card to beat that head start.</li>
        <li><strong>The "Trifecta" Play:</strong> If you also have a Chase Sapphire Preferred or Ink Preferred, you can move the cash back from this card to that card. Suddenly, your 1.5% cash back becomes <strong>1.5x Ultimate Rewards points</strong>, which are worth way more when redeemed for Hyatt or United flights.</li>
    </ol>

    <h3>0% APR: The Inventory Hack</h3>
    <p>The Ink Unlimited comes with <strong>0% Intro APR for 12 months</strong> on purchases. If you need to buy $5,000 of inventory, a new laptop, or a camera for your business, you can put it on this card and pay it off slowly over a year without paying a dime in interest.</p>

    <h3>Ink Unlimited vs. Ink Cash</h3>
    <p>Chase has two no-fee business cards. Get the <strong>Ink Unlimited</strong> if your spending is all over the place (Contractors, Inventory, Rent). Get the <strong>Ink Cash</strong> if you spend a lot on Office Supply Stores or Internet/Phone bills (where it earns 5%).</p>
