    <h2>The Deep Dive</h2>
    <p>The Wells Fargo Reflect doesn't pretend to be fun. It is a financial tool designed to pause interest payments for nearly two years. If you can't pay off your debt in 21 months, you need a bankruptcy lawyer, not a credit card.</p>

    <h3>The Offer (The Extension)</h3>
    <p>You get an 18-month 0% intro APR period by default. If you make your minimum payments on time during that period, Wells Fargo extends it by up to 3 months, for a total of <strong>21 months</strong>.</p>

    <h3>The "5% Fee" Debate</h3>
    <p>Most cards charge a 3% balance transfer fee. The Reflect often charges <strong>5%</strong> ($5 min). Is getting 21 months (vs 15 or 18 months on other cards) worth an extra $100 fee on a $5,000 transfer? If you really need the time, yes. If you can pay it off in 15 months, get the Wells Fargo Active Cash instead (lower fee + rewards).</p>

    <h3>Reflect vs. Simplicity</h3>
    <p>These are the two titans of the 21-month offer. The <strong>Citi Simplicity</strong> usually wins on math because it typically has a lower transfer fee (3%) and no late fees. The <strong>Wells Fargo Reflect</strong> wins on perks because it includes Cell Phone Protection.</p>

    <h3>Who Should Get This Card?</h3>
    <ul>
        <li>You need the absolute maximum time possible (21 months) to pay off debt.</li>
        <li>You want cell phone insurance but don't want to pay an annual fee.</li>
        <li>You already bank with Wells Fargo.</li>
    </ul>
