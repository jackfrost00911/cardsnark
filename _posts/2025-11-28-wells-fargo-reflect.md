## The Deep Dive

The Wells Fargo Reflect doesn't pretend to be fun. It is a financial tool designed to pause interest payments for nearly two years. If you can't pay off your debt in 21 months, you need a bankruptcy lawyer, not a credit card.

### The Offer (The Extension)
You get a **0% intro APR for 21 months** from account opening on purchases and qualifying balance transfers. This is currently one of the longest offers on the market.

### The "5% Fee" Debate
Most cards charge a 3% balance transfer fee. The Reflect charges **5%** ($5 min). Is getting 21 months (vs 15 or 18 months on other cards) worth an extra $100 fee on a $5,000 transfer? If you really need the time, yes. If you can pay it off in 15 months, get the Wells Fargo Active Cash instead (lower fee + rewards).

### Reflect vs. Simplicity
These are the two titans of the 21-month offer. The **Citi Simplicity** usually wins on math because it typically has a lower transfer fee (3% intro) and no late fees. The **Wells Fargo Reflect** wins on perks because it includes Cell Phone Protection.

### Who Should Get This Card?
1.  You need the absolute maximum time possible (21 months) to pay off debt.
2.  You want cell phone insurance but don't want to pay an annual fee.
3.  You already bank with Wells Fargo.
