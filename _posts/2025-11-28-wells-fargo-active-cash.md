    <p>The card for people who are tired of doing math at the checkout counter. It earns 2% cash back on everything, from diamond rings to breakfast burritos.</p>

    <p>The $200 bonus is what sets this apart. Most 2% cards (like the Citi Double Cash) don't give you a bonus. Wells Fargo is paying you $200 just to try it out.</p>

    <h3>Active Cash vs. Citi Double Cash</h3>
    <p>This is the heavyweight title fight of the boring credit card world. Both earn 2% on everything with $0 annual fees. However, Wells Fargo wins on two fronts: It usually has a <strong>$200 Sign-Up Bonus</strong> (Citi rarely does), and it includes <strong>Cell Phone Protection</strong> (Citi stripped most insurance benefits).</p>

    <h3>The Cell Phone Protection (Hidden Gem)</h3>
    <p>This is rare for a no-fee card. If you pay your monthly cell phone bill with the Active Cash, you get up to <strong>$600 of protection</strong> against damage or theft. There is a $25 deductible, which is peanuts compared to the cost of a new iPhone screen.</p>
