    <h2>The Deep Dive</h2>
    <p>The Amex Business Platinum is a status symbol that actually works hard for you‚Äîif you know how to use it. The $695 fee is scary, but the $1,000+ in potential value is undeniable.</p>

    <h3>The Math (Does the fee make sense?)</h3>
    <p>This card only works if you use the credits. Here is the breakdown:</p>
    <ul>
        <li><strong>Annual Fee:</strong> $695</li>
        <li><strong>Dell Credit:</strong> -$400 ($200 twice a year)</li>
        <li><strong>Airline Credit:</strong> -$200 (Incidentals only)</li>
        <li><strong>Wireless Credit:</strong> -$120 ($10/month)</li>
        <li><strong>Net Cost:</strong> <strong style="color: #16a34a;">-$25 (Profit)</strong></li>
    </ul>
    <p><strong>The Verdict:</strong> If your business buys electronics from Dell, pays a phone bill, and flies on airplanes, Amex is paying you to hold this card. If you don't use those credits, you're just lighting $695 on fire.</p>

    <h3>The Secret Weapon: 35% Rebate</h3>
    <p>This feature separates the Business Platinum from the Personal Platinum. If you use Membership Rewards points to book a flight on your selected airline (or ANY business/first class flight) through Amex Travel, you get <strong>35% of those points back</strong>.</p>
    <p><em>Why this matters:</em> Normally, 100,000 points gets you $1,000 of travel. With this rebate, 100,000 points gets you roughly <strong>$1,540</strong> of travel. It makes your points worth 1.54 cents each without dealing with transfer partners.</p>

    <h3>Lounge Access: Is it worth it?</h3>
    <p>If you fly more than 5 times a year, yes. Airport food is expensive. The Centurion Lounge gives you a chef-curated buffet, craft cocktails, and fast Wi-Fi for free. You also get Priority Pass (1,200+ lounges) and Delta SkyClub access (when flying Delta).</p>

    <h3>Who Should Get This Card?</h3>
    <ul>
        <li>You travel for business at least 4-5 times a year.</li>
        <li>You buy computers/electronics from Dell (or can resell them).</li>
        <li>You want elite status at Hilton and Marriott without staying 50 nights.</li>
        <li>You have a huge tax bill (You can earn 1.5x points on purchases over $5,000).</li>
    </ul>

    <h3>Who should skip it?</h3>
    <p>Skip this if you rarely fly or find "coupon clipping" (tracking monthly credits) annoying. Also, do not use this for everyday spending‚Äîthe 1x earning rate is terrible.</p>
