    <h2>The Deep Dive</h2>
    <p>If you're the type who orders DoorDash three times a week, shops at Whole Foods like it's a hobby, and considers brunch a personality trait, this card was designed in a lab specifically for you.</p>

    <h3>The Credit Situation (It's Complicated)</h3>
    <p>The fee is $250, but if you max out the credits, the effective cost drops significantly. Here is the math:</p>
    <ul>
        <li><strong>Annual Fee:</strong> $250</li>
        <li><strong>Uber Cash:</strong> -$120 ($10/month)</li>
        <li><strong>Dining Credit:</strong> -$84 ($7/month)</li>
        <li><strong>Net Annual Cost:</strong> <strong style="color: #0c4a6e;">$46</strong></li>
    </ul>
    <p><strong>The catch:</strong> The $10/month Uber credit doesn't roll over. If you don't use it, it's gone. So your "real" annual fee is somewhere between $46 (if you max it out) and $250 (if you never Uber).</p>

    <h3>How You Earn Points</h3>
    <p>This card is genuinely elite for food spending:</p>
    <ul>
        <li><strong>4x Dining:</strong> Restaurants, bars, cafes, and delivery apps (DoorDash, Uber Eats).</li>
        <li><strong>4x Groceries:</strong> At U.S. supermarkets on up to $25,000 per year.</li>
        <li><strong>3x Flights:</strong> Booked directly with airlines.</li>
        <li><strong>1x Everything Else:</strong> This is not an "everything card."</li>
    </ul>

    <h3>Who Should Get This Card?</h3>
    <ul>
        <li>Foodies who dine out 3+ times per week.</li>
        <li>People who grocery shop at supermarkets (not Costco/Target).</li>
        <li>Uber/Uber Eats users who'll max the $10/month credit.</li>
    </ul>

    <h3>Who should skip it?</h3>
    <p>Skip this card if you shop primarily at Walmart, Target, or Costco, or if you live somewhere Amex isn't widely accepted. Also, if you find tracking monthly credits annoying, the value proposition drops fast.</p>
