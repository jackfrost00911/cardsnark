## The Deep Dive

If you're the type who orders DoorDash three times a week, shops at Whole Foods like it's a hobby, and considers brunch a personality trait, this card was designed in a lab specifically for you.

### The Math (Does the fee make sense?)
The fee is $250, but if you max out the credits, the effective cost drops significantly. Here is the breakdown:

* **Annual Fee:** $250
* **Uber Cash:** -$120 ($10/month)
* **Dining Credit:** -$84 ($7/month)
* **Net Cost:** <strong class="text-blue-800">$46</strong>

**The Verdict:** The catch is that the monthly credits don't roll over. If you don't use them, they are gone. So your "real" annual fee is somewhere between $46 (if you max it out) and $250 (if you never Uber).

### The Secret Weapon: 4x Multipliers
This card is genuinely elite for food spending. While most cards offer 2x or 3x, the Gold Card hits harder:

* **4x Dining:** Restaurants, bars, cafes, and delivery apps (DoorDash, Uber Eats).
* **4x Groceries:** At U.S. supermarkets on up to $25,000 per year.
* **3x Flights:** Booked directly with airlines.

> *Why this matters:* If you spend $500 a month on groceries and $300 on dining, you are earning 3,200 points per month. That's nearly 40,000 points a year just on food you were going to eat anyway.

### Who Should Get This Card?
1. Foodies who dine out 3+ times per week.
2. People who grocery shop at supermarkets (not Costco/Target).
3. Uber/Uber Eats users who will max the $10/month credit.
